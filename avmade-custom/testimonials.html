When selecting a factory for your fashion line, matching your brand's needs with the factory's specialization is vital. For instance, if a factory set up is for couture brands, but you’re launching a contemporary or athleisure line, you can expect costs to soar, often 3-5 times more than standard.

How to tell the difference in factories? In the world of clothing manufacturing, it's important to distinguish between factories that truly understand your needs and those that are simply looking to upsell their services. Starting your clothing brand doesn't have to be a complicated process. The key factor to consider is whether the factory has experience in your specific category.

If the factory specializes in your category, your project should proceed smoothly and efficiently. Quality standards may vary from one factory to another, but if you choose a factory with expertise in your niche, you shouldn't have to break the bank to kickstart your brand.

    To make the right choice, follow these simple steps:
    1. Check the factory's specialization.
    2. Compare their pricing with that of other factories.

CHOOSING SALES CHANNEL

Picking the right model for optimal brand growth and longevity.
You are selling to your customer directly.
Knowing as wholesale.
When launching a brand, your initial focus should be crafting your mission and forging a deep bond with customers. This foundation is crucial for two reasons:

1. Feedback: Direct customer relationships offer invaluable insights to refine your product. Wholesalers, despite their best intentions, are not capable of delivering the candid feedback you need.
2. Brand Ambassadors: Loyal customers will champion your brand, fostering organic growth and network effect.

It might seem ideal to dive into wholesale for what seems like a good sales channel, but it's not for startups. Wholesale as an emerging brand can erode your margins by minus 60-70%. Remember, established brands resort to wholesale once they've maximized their direct-to-consumer potential. Plus, established brands can do large volumes and therefore they get greater manufacturing cost reductions to compensate for their margin. As a startup, prioritize building your foundation. Jumping ahead to wholesale prematurely risks both financial and emotional burnout, jeopardizing your brand's longevity."

DO’s & DON’Ts

Due diligence is critical, don’t rush it.
  • Choose a factory specializing in your niche.
  • Research and compare factories.
  • Look for available incentives.
  • Stay away from factories claiming expertise in both couture and contemporary manufacturing.
  • Manufacturing is a very complex business. Stay away from factories offering services beyond manufacturing, such as marketing, fulfillment, etc.
  • Avoid factories that won't provide a full-package cost breakdown from development to production before signing a contract.
  • Startups must avoid overseas factories.
